About Poison Ivy

15%

Poison ivy is a plant native to North America, commonly found in forests, fields, and urban areas. It produces an oily resin called urushiol, which triggers allergic reactions in most people.

Key Characteristics

  • Thrives in forests, fields, and urban areas
  • Produces urushiol, an allergenic oil
  • Recognizable by clusters of three pointed leaves
  • Contact with plant, sap, or contaminated objects can cause exposure

Symptom Timeline

0-2 Hours After Exposure

No visible reaction yet. Wash affected area with soap and water within 10-15 minutes. Clean all exposed items.

12-48 Hours After Exposure

Redness and mild swelling begin. Rash appears with increasing itchiness.

2-7 Days After Exposure

Rash worsens, developing into fluid-filled blisters. Itchiness intensifies.

1-2 Weeks After Exposure

Rash begins to crust over and heal. Watch for signs of infection.

Treatment Options

  • Wash affected skin thoroughly with soap and water
  • Apply calamine lotion or hydrocortisone cream
  • Use cool compresses for relief
  • Take antihistamines like Benadryl for itching
  • Use oatmeal baths or aloe vera for soothing
  • Keep blisters clean and dry

Where to Get Help

Emergency Contacts

Poison Control Center: 1-800-222-1222 (24/7 Hotline)

American Academy of Dermatology: (888) 462-DERM (3376)

Seek Immediate Medical Attention If:

  • Rash spreads to sensitive areas (eyes, mouth, genitals)
  • Signs of infection appear (pus, yellow scabs, fever)
  • You experience severe swelling or difficulty breathing
  • Rash covers large portions of your body
